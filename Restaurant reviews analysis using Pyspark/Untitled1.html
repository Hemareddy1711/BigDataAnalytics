## Dataset Insights – Restaurant Reviews

The dataset provided contains *500 customer reviews* collected from various restaurants. It includes the columns: ReviewID, RestaurantName, City, Rating, ReviewerAge, and ReviewText. The dataset is *balanced and clean*, containing no missing values, ensuring reliability for analysis and modeling.

### 1. Structure and Uniqueness
The dataset comprises *500 rows* and *6 columns. Each record represents a **unique customer review*, identified by its ReviewID.  
- RestaurantName contains *20 unique restaurants*.  
- City has *10 unique locations*.  
- Ratings vary between *1.0 and 5.0*.  
- ReviewerAge ranges from 18 to 65 years.  

The dataset ensures *diversity across restaurants and cities* and captures customer feedback reliably.

### 2. Restaurant and City Distribution
- The dataset covers *20 restaurants* with reviews distributed across *10 cities*.  
- Each restaurant has multiple reviews across different cities, providing *geographical diversity*.  
- Balanced representation ensures *fair sentiment analysis* and modeling without bias toward a single restaurant or city.

### 3. Rating Insights
- Ratings range from *1.0 to 5.0, with an average of around **3.0*.  
- Median ratings are close to the mean, suggesting a fairly balanced distribution.  
- Some restaurants show higher ratings, indicating customer preference and satisfaction.

### 4. Review Length and Text Analysis
- Reviews vary in length from *3–40 words, with an average around **12–15 words*.  
- Short reviews are often emotionally strong, e.g., “Loved it!” or “Very bad food.”  
- Longer reviews provide detailed feedback for *aspect-based sentiment analysis*.

### 5. Age Distribution of Reviewers
- Reviewer ages range from *18 to 65 years, with an average of *~35 years**.  
- Most reviewers are in the *mid-career age range*, ensuring diverse perspectives in feedback.

### 6. Key Observations
- *Positive Reviews:* Often include words like delicious, excellent, friendly, tasty.  
- *Negative Reviews:* Include words like slow service, tasteless, rude, cold food.  
- Frequent words like “food,” “service,” and “place” appear across many reviews.  

### 7. Usefulness for Analysis
- Suitable for *sentiment classification* (Positive/Negative).  
- Useful for *text preprocessing, **feature extraction, and **model building* (Logistic Regression, SVM, CNN, LSTM).  
- Helps analyze *customer satisfaction trends* for different restaurants and cities.

### 8. Summary
The *Restaurant Reviews dataset* provides a *balanced, diverse, and clean corpus* for sentiment analysis. It captures customer feedback effectively, making it ideal for *educational purposes and real-world NLP applications* such as chatbots, review summarizers, or restaurant feedback analyzers.

from pyspark.sql.functions import when

# Increase ratings by 0.5 for reviewers older than 45 (max 5.0)
df = df.withColumn(
    "UpdatedRating",
    when(col("ReviewerAge") > 45,
         when(col("Rating") + 0.5 > 5.0, 5.0).otherwise(col("Rating") + 0.5))
    .otherwise(col("Rating"))
)

Conclusion – Restaurant Reviews Dataset¶

The dataset provides insights into customer feedback across 500 reviews for 20 restaurants in 10 cities. It reflects a **balanced distribution of sentiments, with roughly equal numbers of positive and negative reviews, making it ideal for sentiment analysis.

  • The ratings range from 1 to 5, showing varied customer satisfaction levels across restaurants.
  • Reviewers’ ages span from 18 to 65, capturing perspectives from both young and experienced customers.
  • Short reviews are often emotionally strong, while longer reviews provide detailed feedback about food, service, and ambiance.
  • The dataset is clean and consistent, with no missing values, making it suitable for machine learning and NLP applications.

Overall, the dataset is perfect for:

  • Sentiment classification models
  • Text preprocessing and feature extraction exercises
  • Analyzing restaurant performance and customer satisfaction trends

It can be used to build predictive models, understand **customer preferences, and derive actionable insights for restaurant operations and marketing strategies.
